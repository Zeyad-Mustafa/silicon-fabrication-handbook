## How to Use This Database

### For Students
Start with the "Classic" papers to understand historical context, then move to recent reviews for current state-of-the-art.

### For Researchers
Use DOIs to access papers through institutional subscriptions. Check citation counts to identify influential work.

### For Industry
Focus on conference proceedings (IEDM, VLSI Symp) for latest manufacturing techniques.

### Citation Format

When citing papers from this database in your work:

```bibtex
@article{example2003,
  author = {Author, A. and Coauthor, B.},
  title = {Paper Title},
  journal = {Journal Name},
  year = {2003},
  volume = {50},
  pages = {123-145},
  doi = {10.xxxx/xxxxx}
}
```
